Human Trafficking Awareness

Education saves lives. Awareness creates change.

Human trafficking does not always look the way people expect.

What Is Human Trafficking?

Human trafficking is the exploitation of a person through force, fraud, coercion, manipulation, or control for another person's benefit.

It can happen to people of any age, race, gender, religion, nationality, or socioeconomic background.

Many victims never realize what is happening until much later because trafficking often begins with trust, opportunity, relationships, promises, dependency, or emotional manipulation.

Common Myths

❌ Myth

Human trafficking always involves kidnapping.


✅ Reality

Most trafficking situations involve manipulation, coercion, dependency, and control rather than abduction.

❌ Myth

Victims can simply leave.


✅ Reality

Fear, trauma, threats, emotional dependence, finances, immigration concerns, or isolation may prevent someone from leaving.

❌ Myth

Trafficking only happens to strangers.


✅ Reality

Traffickers are often people victims know, trust, admire, or depend on.

Warning Signs of Trafficking

Isolation

Being cut off from friends, family, support systems, or outside communication.

Control

Someone controlling transportation, housing, money, schedules, or communication.

Fear

Being afraid of upsetting or leaving a particular person.

Dependency

Feeling like survival depends on one individual.

Threats

Threats involving finances, housing, reputation, immigration status, or safety.

Manipulation

Constant guilt, pressure, emotional abuse, or psychological control.

Sex Trafficking & Labor Trafficking

Sex Trafficking

Involves exploitation for commercial sexual activity through force, fraud, coercion, or manipulation.

Labor Trafficking

Involves exploiting someone for labor or services through coercion, threats, manipulation, debt, or abuse of power.

Things People Don't Talk About

Missing Your Trafficker

Trauma bonds can make survivors miss the person who harmed them.

Questioning Yourself

Many survivors wonder whether what happened was "serious enough."

Physical Health Problems

Trauma can impact the body, not just the mind.

Feeling Alone

Healing often feels isolating, but survivors are never truly alone.

Online Safety for Teens

Many traffickers use social media, messaging apps, gaming platforms, and online communities to build trust and manipulate potential victims.

Protect Personal Information

Be Careful With Online Relationships

Tell A Trusted Adult If Something Feels Wrong

Trust Your Instincts

For Parents

Open communication is one of the most powerful protective tools.

Create an environment where children feel safe discussing uncomfortable situations without fear of punishment or judgment.

Awareness, education, and trust can make a significant difference.
